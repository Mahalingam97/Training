CRICKET FAQ'S

What is super over?

In a Super Over, each team bats for one additional over after the match ends in a tie. A Super Over, also known as one-over eliminator, is a method used in limited-overs cricket matches to decide the winner in case of a tie

What is no ball rule in cricket?

A No-ball is a delivery which does not count as one of the bowler's six legitimate balls in one over.The umpire will call a no ball by raising an arm at shoulder height and the fielding team must bowl another legitimate delivery.

When 60 over is reduced to 50 over?

It was a stunning victory in the history of cricket. In the 1987 World Cup, the ICC decided to reduce the overs from 60 to 50 and it was a huge success.
